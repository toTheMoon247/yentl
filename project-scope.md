# Human-Centered Dating Platform

## Problem

Modern dating apps are frustrating for both men and women.

Women often receive endless matches and messages, making the experience overwhelming and low quality.

At the same time, many men spend hours swiping without getting meaningful matches at all.

This creates a broken experience where:

* Women feel overloaded
* Men feel invisible
* Conversations go nowhere
* Very few matches turn into real dates

Many dating apps also place important features behind expensive paywalls while still failing to deliver real results.

As a result, users often feel that dating apps are designed to maximize profit and screen time rather than helping people genuinely connect.

---

## Positioning

Yentl is for the broad dating market — positioned directly against Tinder and Hinge as **the app that actually gets you matched**.

The promise is outcome-based, not lifestyle-based: users come to Yentl because other apps haven't delivered real matches, and they want one that will.

---

## Solution

Build a dating platform focused on creating real matches and real dates.

Users can swipe and match like in traditional dating apps, but the platform also includes a human matchmaking team working behind the scenes.

The matchmaking team helps ensure that users are consistently given genuine opportunities to match with compatible people.

After matching, both users have 24 hours to confirm whether they want to go on a real date.

If both users confirm:

* The match becomes a confirmed date.

If one user rejects **or ignores** the date (no response within 24 hours):

* That user moves lower in the matchmaking queue.
* The user who was rejected keeps high priority and is matched again.

Ignoring a match is treated the same as rejecting it. This keeps the system simple and rewards users who show up for the people they've matched with.

This system rewards people who are serious about dating while ensuring that active users are not endlessly ignored.

The goal is to create a dating platform that feels fair, intentional, and focused on real-world results.

---

## Core Product Philosophy

The platform is designed to encourage:

* Real dates
* Serious participation
* Fair matchmaking
* Better conversations
* Genuine opportunities for all users

Instead of:

* Endless swiping
* Fake engagement
* Match hoarding
* Paywalls without results
* Time wasting

---

## Business Model

Yentl charges a **per-confirmed-date fee**: the platform is free to use until a date is mutually confirmed by both users, at which point a fee is collected.

This aligns revenue directly with the outcome Yentl exists to deliver — real dates — and avoids the pattern of paywalling features that don't actually produce results.

Open questions to resolve before launch:

* Who pays the fee — one side, both, or split
* The fee amount
* How to discourage "confirm-then-ghost" behavior intended to game the system
* Refund or dispute policy when a confirmed date doesn't happen
